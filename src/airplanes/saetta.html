The Saetta found its way to my work bench when I put together a large scale MC.202 Folgore that had been in my to-build stash for more than a decade. That Folgore turned out to be a true dog of a kit, and rather than sending it to sleep with the fishes, I opted to keep it as a test-painting platform. But I was in the middle of my "Year of the Axis" build, and I had done a number of Japanese and German aircraft to that point. I felt I needed the third mostly useless branch of the Axis, the Italians, and I needed a replacement Folgore. When I found this Saetta, I decided that one would look good on my shelf.
